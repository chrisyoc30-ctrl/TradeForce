# TradeScore — Support response procedures

Guidelines for anyone handling **hello@tradescore.uk** (or the live support inbox). Adjust to your internal tooling (Help Scout, Zendesk, shared Gmail, etc.).

---

## 1. Principles

- **Target:** first response within **24 hours** on business days (set expectations with auto-reply if you’re smaller — the support-received email already promises this).
- **Tone:** professional, helpful, friendly, action-oriented, reassuring.
- **Safety:** never ask for full card numbers or passwords. Verify account ownership using email match or internal tools before sharing sensitive lead/payment detail.

---

## 2. Triage and priority

| Priority | Examples | Response |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **P1 — Urgent** | Payment taken incorrectly, security concern, legal threat, media | Same day; escalate to management immediately. |
| **P2 — High** | Can’t access paid lead, dispute opened, repeated failed payments | Same day where possible; < 24h first response. |
| **P3 — Normal** | How fees work, profile edits, general product questions | < 24h first response. |
| **P4 — Low** | Feature requests, feedback | < 48h or batch weekly. |

---

## 3. Standard workflow

1. **Acknowledge** — send or ensure the “support ticket received” email fired; if manual, reply with ticket ID.
2. **Classify** — billing, product, dispute, technical, abuse.
3. **Investigate** — internal logs, Stripe dashboard, lead ID, user email.
4. **Resolve** — clear steps, links, screenshots if helpful.
5. **Close** — send “resolved” summary; invite follow-up if incomplete.
6. **Log** — note outcome for repeat issues and refunds policy.

---

## 4. Escalation

**Escalate to management / founder when:**

- Refund above your delegated limit (define e.g. **> £50** or **any goodwill > £25**).
- Legal, regulatory, or ICO-related wording.
- Threat of chargeback **and** unclear fault.
- Harassment, discrimination, or safety risk.
- Suspected fraud or account takeover.
- Press or partnership inquiry.

**Escalation package:** ticket ID, user emails, lead/payment IDs, timeline, what was promised, recommended action.

---

## 5. Common issues and sample directions

*(Tune copy to your actual Terms and product behaviour.)*

| Issue | Direction |
|-------|-----------|
| “What does an accepted lead cost?” | Explain **£25 per accepted lead** (and **first lead may be free** if that applies to their account). Link FAQ/pricing. |
| “Payment failed” | Ask them to retry; suggest another card; check bank. Link retry URL if you have it. Escalate if Stripe shows repeated failures. |
| “I didn’t mean to accept” | Check Terms and time window. If within a **short cooling-off** policy you define, consider refund; otherwise explain binding acceptance. |
| “Lead was fake / wrong number” | Gather evidence; validate against logs; **refund or credit** if lead materially misrepresented (per your policy). |
| “Homeowner never replied” | Clarify TradeScore connects parties; cannot guarantee homeowner behaviour; no refund unless policy says so for non-contact. |
| “Delete my data” | Acknowledge GDPR request; confirm identity; forward to whoever handles erasure; complete within legal timelines. |

---

## 6. Refunds — when to offer

**Consider full or partial refund when:**

- Duplicate charge or **proven billing error**.
- Lead **materially not as described** and your policy covers it.
- **Service failure** on TradeScore side (e.g. payment succeeded but lead not unlocked due to bug).

**Generally do not refund when:**

- User changed mind after **clear acceptance** and work was purely “didn’t win the job.”
- Dispute not yet investigated — use dispute workflow first.

Always document **reason code** in your finance notes.

---

## 7. Disputes between homeowner and tradesperson

- Stay **neutral**; gather both sides.
- Point to **Terms** (dispute resolution, liability limits).
- Set **deadlines** for responses (mirror dispute email copy).
- Escalate if threats, injury, or property damage alleged.

---

## 8. Quality checklist (before send)

- [ ] Correct **ticket / lead / payment** IDs.
- [ ] **Next step** is obvious (link or bullet).
- [ ] Tone calm; no blame.
- [ ] No promises you can’t keep (legal, refunds).
- [ ] Internal note updated.

---

## 9. After-hours and volume spikes

- Set inbox **auto-reply** with hours and emergency path for security.
- If SLA cannot be met, **batch** honest updates (“we’re experiencing high volume”).

---

*Internal playbook — not legal advice. Align refund and dispute rules with your Terms of Service and counsel.*
